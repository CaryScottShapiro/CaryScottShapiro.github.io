Favorite Moments

Below are a few of Cary's favorite moments. They would all be included in a proper eulogy.

Bungee Jump - The summer after Middle School, Cary went on a class trip to South Africa. The group visited the bridge that's in Guinness Book of World Records for highest bungee jump. Cary was the first in the group to jump because he is the bravest... and because the order was determined by weight.

School Week - During a one-week period in highschool, Cary placed in a national Constitutional Law competition, was cast as the lead in the school play (Tevye in Fiddler, obviously), and was accepted early to Northwestern University. This was a notable week, even for Cary.

Pilot - During college, one of Cary's fraternity brothers earned his pilot's license and offered to fly Cary to Wisconsin in a two-passenger airplane. During the flight, Cary was allowed to hold the wheel and control the plane for several seconds. Cary is now technically a pilot. And both flyers are technically felons.

First Job - Upon graduation from Northwestern University, Cary was giving campus tours and telling groups that he couln't find a job. One of the father's asked for Cary's resume. The father turned out to be the CEO of Boeing. Cary has worked at Boeing for the last seven years.

Coding - During graduate school, Cary learned how to code and create his own website. It is the very site you're readin now. How meta.
